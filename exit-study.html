How many Tor exits get captcha'd?

Scraping 3,000 random Smyths products/day across every region (UK · IE · DE · AT · CH · FR · NL) over Tor, for 3 days — measuring how often an exit hits the captcha wall, with real confidence intervals. Solver limited to qwen3-vl-32b + shape-match + color-cv.

Why this exists

The captcha only fires on low-trust connections. The earlier number (16.5%) came from just 97 fetches — a ±7% guess. This run gathers thousands of real fetches across hundreds of exits so the captcha rate, the block rate, and the share of hostile exits are measured properly, not estimated from a handful.

Each fetch is the real browser flow (clears Imperva's sensor). “Captcha” = the exit threw the doodle wall; “block” = a hard Imperva denial; “clean” = product loaded with no challenge. Ranges are 95% confidence intervals (Wilson).
